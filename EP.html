How pollution is effecting the Human Civilation

Health Related Issues

Pollution is severely impacting human civilization by harming health, the environment, and the economy. Air pollution leads to respiratory diseases, heart problems, and even cancer, while water pollution causes deadly illnesses and contaminates drinking sources. Soil pollution reduces food quality, affecting nutrition and health. Pollution is also a major driver of climate change, resulting in extreme weather, rising sea levels, and natural disasters that displace communities and damage infrastructure. Ecosystems are being destroyed, leading to biodiversity loss, which in turn affects agriculture and natural balance. Economically, billions are spent on healthcare and disaster recovery. Poor and vulnerable populations suffer the most, deepening inequality and reducing quality of life. Pollution not only shortens life expectancy but also contributes to mental health issues due to constant exposure to toxic environments.
